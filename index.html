About Andrew
Brodsky.

My name is Andrew Brodsky. Growing up in New York City all my life, I have become accustomed to dealing with uncomfortable situations and looking for the opportune moment to make an impact. This high-stress environment has taught me how to cope with problems and think through them quickly and analytically.

I am all about efficiency, and I constantly strive to maximize it in all areas of my life. My main interest is in real estate. Walking around New York City, I see countless buildings unused and underutilized, and it causes physical pain. I want to maximize the efficiency of the market and build beautiful buildings that increase the quality of everyday life for everyone in the city.

I see a homeless person down the street from an abandoned building, and I understand how that is a result of market inefficiencies and the failure to allocate resources. Especially under the context of the New York housing crisis, I strive to spend my life trying to make a difference. I fight that problem weekly working closely with Grassroots Grocery, an organization that combats food insecurity.
